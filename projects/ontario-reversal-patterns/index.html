The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 01 of 09
Greenbelt
Land Removals
Decision
Nov 4, 2022: Province removes 2,994 hectares from Greenbelt protection. Internal process later found to have favoured specific developers.
Stated: accelerate housing, add land supply.
Reversal
AG and Integrity Commissioner both find process favoured developers. Ministers resign. RCMP opens investigation. September 2023: Ford reverses, promises to return all land.
AG · Integrity Commissioner · public · RCMP
"I broke that promise."
10 months

The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 02 of 09
Dissolution of
Peel Region
Decision
June 2023: Hazel McCallion Act passed. Dissolves Peel Region by 2025. Transition board established.
Stated: modernization, efficiency, local autonomy.
Reversal
Municipalities raise concerns about financial and operational complexity. Transition costs unresolved. December 2023: legislation introduced to undo the dissolution.
Peel municipalities · local leaders · public scrutiny
Claim: acted on "feedback" about cost and complexity.
6 months

The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 03 of 09
Education Workers &
Notwithstanding Clause
Decision
Nov 2022: Legislation imposes contract on 55,000 CUPE workers, bans strikes, invokes notwithstanding clause to pre-empt Charter challenges.
Stated: maintain in-person schooling, avoid disruption.
Reversal
Workers strike despite the law. Allied unions signal broader action. Government offers to repeal if workers end strike. Within weeks: law withdrawn.
CUPE strike · allied unions · public scrutiny of notwithstanding use

The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 04 of 09
Bill 124 —
Public Sector Wage Cap
Decision
2019: Bill 124 caps public-sector wages at 1% annually for three years. Unions immediately begin court challenges.
Stated: control compensation growth, manage provincial finances.
Reversal
Superior Court and Court of Appeal both rule the law unconstitutional — infringes collective bargaining rights. Feb 2024: government will not appeal. Law to be repealed.
Union court challenges · adverse rulings at two court levels
Claim: cost-of-living pressures on workers.
5 years

The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 05 of 09
Blue
Licence Plates
Decision
2019: New blue Ontario licence plates introduced as part of provincial rebrand following procurement and design decisions.
Stated: more durable plates, updated provincial branding.
Reversal
Police report plates are difficult to read at night. Reports and images confirm visibility issues. Government quickly halts rollout. ~170,000 blue plates remain in circulation as of 2023.
Front-line police flagging safety · media coverage
Quickly

The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 06 of 09
Public Health &
Municipal Funding Cuts
Decision
2019: Province cuts cost-sharing to municipalities for public health and other local services. Changes are retroactive, affecting already-set budgets.
Stated: fiscal restraint, rebalancing provincial-municipal cost sharing.
Reversal
Mayors organize collective opposition. Mid-year retroactive cuts cancelled. Broader cuts paused during pandemic. 2023: government permanently reverses the cost-sharing changes.
Coordinated municipal opposition · COVID-19 exposing public health capacity needs
2019 – 2023

The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 07 of 09
Pandemic
Enforcement Powers
Decision
April 2021: Emergency orders expand police powers to stop individuals and close playgrounds as part of COVID-19 response.
Stated: reduce mobility and contacts to control spread.
Reversal
Civil liberties groups and experts criticize the measures. Several police services publicly state they will not use the expanded stop powers. Within days: playgrounds reopened, powers rolled back.
Civil liberties groups · police refusal to enforce · expert and public criticism
"We got it wrong."
Days

Urban Boundary
Expansions
Decision
Province uses ministerial powers to expand urban boundaries and override parts of municipal official plans across multiple regions.
Stated: increase land supply to support provincial housing goals.
Reversal
Municipalities argue extra land is unnecessary. Concerns raised about sprawl and servicing costs. Fall 2023: Minister reverses some boundary expansions, restoring local plans.
Municipal governments · planning staff · media attention to pattern of interventions

The Delusioneers The Pattern · Case 09 of 09
The Government
Jet
Decision
Province purchases a $28.9M Bombardier Challenger 650. Purchase completed and disclosed retroactively — the public learned of it after the fact.
Stated: efficiency in official travel; secure transport including U.S. trips on tariff and investment matters.
Reversal
Media reports the purchase. Opposition labels it a "gravy plane." Backlash intensifies over cost during an affordability crisis. Within ~48 hours: Ford announces the province will sell the aircraft.
Media coverage · opposition parties · public backlash on cost
"Now is not the right time."
48 hours
